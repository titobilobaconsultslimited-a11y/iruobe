🌍 Our Heritage

One Ingredient. Many Names.

The locust bean has been at the heart of West African cooking for centuries — known by different names, loved by one continent.

Yoruba — Nigeria
Irú
The foundation of Yoruba soups — ewedu, efo, egusi, and okra
Igbo — Nigeria
Ogiri
Essential for Ofe Onugbu, Oha, and Nsala soups
Hausa/North — Nigeria & Niger
Dawadawa
Used in miyan kuka, taushe, and groundnut soup
Ghana & Côte d'Ivoire
Soumbala
Core ingredient in Ghanaian and Ivorian stews
Senegal & Gambia
Nététou
Used in thieboudienne and yassa for depth of flavour
Benue / Idoma — Nigeria
Okpehe
Central to Idoma and Tiv traditional cooking
